Harmony offers a lot more than just Sunday services and children's RE classes. Here are some of the many ways to experience Harmony:

  • Family-friendly social gatherings: movie matinees, potlucks, Trunk-or-Treat, Santa's Workshop, etc.
  • Nonfiction book discussion groups
  • Circle Suppers: rotating small group dinners
  • Adult Religious Exploration: small group discussions
  • Youth group outings

Harmony members, friends, and visitors are welcome to attend all events unless they are marked specifically as “Members Only”.

Sunday, September 4 at 10:00 am - Sunday Service (Childcare Provided)
"It’s in the Book: Understanding the Bible as non-dogmatic wisdom, and the expanded version of our UU principles" by Dick Galloway - Because of the ways religion has been abused, many UUs are resistant to Biblical/religious language. We’re going to break some “Biblical idols” to rediscover its original meaning and purpose as guiding wisdom for life’s path before it was turned into a dogmatic system of beliefs.

Sunday, September 18 at 10:00 am - Sunday Service (Childcare Provided)
"Living the Seven Principles" by Carrie Rogan and the Sequoia Class - UU's are united, in part, by 7 Principles; they guide us, acknowledge our history, and provide us a form of legitimacy. But are we actively practicing these principles? Is that a necessary component to being UU? The Sequoia class will explore these questions and offer ideas about practicing UU. We will also hold our RE Graduation and annual Water Communion ceremonies during the service. Please bring water from your summer travels!

Sunday, October 16 at 10:00 am - Sunday Service (Childcare Provided)
"Respect" by Paul Smith - What do these four scenes have in common: 1) a Muslim man from Saudi Arabia registering for a Feminism class at a university in Illinois, 2) a deaf and mentally handicapped boy walking down the hallway at his school in tears because of his crayons, 3) a college sophomore being yelled at on the street by a stranger for not walking on the proper side of his girlfriend, and 4) an American businessman stopped midday on the busiest street in Bangkok, Thailand for talking too loud? Answer: They all have something important to teach us about showing respect to others. And you’ll hear all their stories on Oct. 16.
